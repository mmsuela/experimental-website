Guidance for Businesses and Employers

U.S. Small Business Administration(SBA): Small businesses and nonprofits can apply for SBA disaster assistance programs online or seek assistance from SBAâ€™s Customer Service Center at 1-800-569-2955/1-800-877-8339 (TTY). For more information: https://disasterloan.sba.gov/ela/

Business Eviction Moratorium: On Aug. 31, 2020, the State of California passed a statewide eviction moratorium The Tenant Relief Act of 2020, commonly known as Assembly Bill 3088.

AB 3088 offers several protections for renters facing hardships during COVID-19 such as,

  • No tenant can be evicted before Feb. 1, 2021.
  • Tenants are allowed 15 days to provide a declaration of hardship to stop the eviction.

Paycheck Protection Program: The PPP is managed by the U.S. Department of the Treasury which provides eligiblity to small businesses and nonprofits organizations, as well as individuals who are self-employed or are independent contractors. Funds are provided in the form of forgiven or deferred loans to be used for payroll costs, interest on mortgages, rent, utilities. Forgiveness is based on the employer maintaining or rehiring employees and maintaining salary levels. More info here: https://www.sba.gov. If you want to take out a loan, click here: https://bit.ly/2xGIBqP

Cash-strapped businesses: The Northern California Financial Development Corporation focuses on assisting businesses with financing in underserved communities. During the COVID-19 pandemic, Nor-Cal FDC has partnered with local financial corporations to provide direct loans for those struggling with cash flow.

Partial Claims: Partial claims, handled the State of California's Employment Development Department, are for employees whose employers want to keep them but have temporarily laid them off or reduced their hours. For more info: https://bit.ly/3934kpB
